The Story: Friends, Travel, and Romance

Based on Emily Henry's 2021 novel, the movie follows Poppy and Alex, best friends who take annual vacations together despite their opposite personalities. After a fallout, they reunite for one last trip to Croatia, where old feelings surface. The film captures the book's witty banter and emotional depth, with stunning locations adding visual appeal.

Why It's Trending in the Philippines

Released on December 25, 2025, the movie quickly climbed Netflix's top 10 in the Philippines, fueled by word-of-mouth and TikTok clips. Filipinos, known for loving rom-coms like "Hello, Love, Goodbye," relate to the travel themes amid a tourism boom. New year resolutions for adventures amplify its relevance.

Key Differences from the Book

The adaptation condenses the timeline, focusing on the final trip while flashing back to past vacations. Some subplots are trimmed for pacing, but core elements like the slow-burn romance remain. Fans note added humor and visual gags, enhancing the cinematic experience.

Cast Performances: Chemistry That Shines

Tom Blyth (Alex) and Emily Bader (Poppy) deliver charming performances, with their banter stealing scenes. Supporting cast, including Mary Steenburgen, adds warmth. Filipino viewers praise the relatable dynamics, drawing parallels to local films.

Soundtrack Highlights: Tunes for Wanderlust

The soundtrack features indie pop like songs from Taylor Swift and Harry Styles, perfectly suiting the vacation vibes. Original tracks enhance emotional moments, with the playlist trending on Spotify in the Philippines.

Cultural Resonance in the Philippines

The film's themes of friendship and self-discovery resonate with Filipinos' value on relationships and travel. Amid economic recovery, it inspires budget-friendly trips, sparking trends like #PWMOVInspiredVacay.

Conclusion: A Must-Watch Rom-Com

'People We Meet on Vacation' trends for its heartwarming story and escapism. Stream on Netflix for your next feel-good watch.
